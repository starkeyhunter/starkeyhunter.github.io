Portfolio

Digit Recognition Using Python

In this study, we aim to develop a machine learning model for recognizing and classifying written digits from images. We use python libraries such as scikit-learn and OpenCV to implement the model and evaluate its performance. The model is trained on a dataset of labeled images of digits, and is then tested on a separate dataset to evaluate its ability to generalize to new data. The output of the model is the predicted digit for each input image.

We compare the performance of our model to other methods for digit recognition, and discuss the strengths and limitations of our approach. The results show that our model is able to achieve high accuracy on the test dataset, demonstrating the effectiveness of machine learning for digit recognition tasks. This project highlights the potential of python and machine learning for solving real-world problems in the field of pattern recognition.

Hospital Time Series Analysis: ETL Development and Control Methods

In this study, we aim to analyze the arrival times of patients at a healthcare facility. We first create a database using SQL to store and organize the time series data of patient arrival times. We then use statistical analysis and charting tools in R to evaluate the data and identify patterns and trends in the arrival times.

The results of our analysis provide insights into the dynamics of patient arrival times at the healthcare facility, and can be used to inform decision-making and planning related to staffing and resource allocation. We compare our findings to previous studies on patient arrival times, and discuss the implications of our results for the healthcare industry. This project demonstrates the potential of combining SQL and R for analyzing time series data in the context of healthcare.
